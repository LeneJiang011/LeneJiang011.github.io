GAN-based VQA

Abstract
Visual Question Answering (VQA) requires reasoning over visual elements and general knowledge to infer the correct answer. In this thesis, we first present a rough review of existing VQA methods and propose the promising significance of introducing scene graphs to the VQA process. Then we present a thorough review of generative adversarial networks (GAN) and point out GAN’s potential in extending scene graphs. The major contribution of this thesis lies in proposing a new training method to improve GAN’s performance on scene graph-based VQA. Our training method draws an analogy between GAN and reinforcement learning, contributing to improve the generator’s performance. Besides, we also propose therotical derivation to build an end-to-end scene graph-based VQA process; Whereas, the implementation is left for future work.

The above abstract is the reduced version of the abstract I submitted in 2018.
In 2021, I read my undergraduate thesis again and found numerous mistakes.
To list some, I failed to make the logic right that what I had done did not solve the problem I proposed. Also, at that time, I failed to break down the problem to attainable ones. As a result, the contribution of the whole thesis looks quite empty.
Besides, the implementation was not clever that many steps were unnecessary. At that time, those steps did take much time that I even had not have time to revise my writings.
Here I am in 2021, I am feeling grateful for my undergraduate supervisor and the committee. At that time, they did not point out those obvious mistakes to beat me down. Instead, they encouraged me to keep releasing my imagination and trying every approach comes to my mind.
In all, though I failed to do well in my undergraduate thesis, what I had done is worth commemorating. ☺︎
